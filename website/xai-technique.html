XAI Techniques

Explainable AI (XAI) provides transparency into AI decisions using various methods. These techniques help make complex models interpretable, increasing trust and usability in real-world applications.

Feature Attribution Methods

These techniques identify which features influence a model‚Äôs decision the most.

  • SHAP: Distributes contribution scores to input features.
  • LIME: Builds simple models to approximate complex AI decisions.
  • Feature Importance: Highlights which features impact predictions.

Example: In healthcare, SHAP can show that high cholesterol and blood pressure contribute to an AI-based heart disease prediction.

Example-Based Explanations

AI decisions are explained by comparing them to past similar cases.

  • Case-Based Reasoning: Uses previous cases to justify new decisions.
  • K-Nearest Neighbors (KNN): Predicts based on the most similar past examples.

Example: In legal AI, case-based reasoning helps predict outcomes by referencing past court rulings.

Surrogate Models

Simpler models approximate complex AI to provide explanations.

  • Decision Tree Surrogates: Uses decision trees to mimic deep learning models.
  • Linear Regression Surrogates: Provides simplified interpretations of black-box models.

Example: In finance, a decision tree surrogate can explain why a loan application was denied.

Visualization-Based Techniques

Graphical methods illustrate how AI models process inputs.

  • Saliency Maps: Highlights key areas in an image affecting predictions.
  • Activation Maps: Shows how deep learning layers respond to inputs.

Example: In medical imaging, AI-generated heatmaps highlight abnormalities in X-rays for doctors.
